Protocol Overview
Purpose

This protocol is a structured framework for improving functional stability in adrenomyeloneuropathy by reducing biological stress on vulnerable neural pathways.

What It Is Not

It does not claim a cure, reversal of established damage, or forced regeneration. The working target is margin: preserving what remains and reducing secondary injury.

Why This Is Different

Many treatments reduce symptoms downstream without changing the underlying strain. This approach focuses on upstream environment optimization: oxidative load, metabolic reliability, and inflammatory pressure. The aim is to reduce background instability rather than temporarily suppress expression.

How We Know Whether It Helps

The model relies on repeated, standardized checks compared against baseline. Functional test outputs are tracked alongside symptom domains such as pain, stiffness, fatigue, and sleep disruption. Changes are interpreted over weeks, not days, and are considered meaningful only when directionally consistent across more than one measure. Contextual variables such as stress, illness, GI upset, and medication adjustments are logged to reduce false attribution.

Protocol Development & Validation

PROTOCOL DEVELOPMENT, VALIDATION, AND STRUCTURAL FRAMEWORK

Conceptual Foundation and Mechanistic Framework

This project was not assembled casually. It represents a structured, multi-layered effort conducted over an extended period of time to address a condition where large-scale translational research remains limited and long-term human trials are scarce.

This protocol is structured around a dual-mechanism model targeting two core drivers of pathology: redox instability and lipid dysregulation. The redox axis is supported through coordinated use of alpha-lipoic acid, N-acetylcysteine, and vitamin E to stabilize oxidative stress and preserve axonal integrity. In parallel, Nervonic Acid is incorporated as a lipid-modulating component to support membrane composition and potentially influence very long chain fatty acid dynamics. Together, these components define the mechanistic foundation of the protocol.

The following methodological steps were undertaken to construct this framework responsibly:

Within this framework, the term "dual mechanism" reflects the recognition that oxidative destabilization and lipid compositional imbalance represent distinct but interacting pathological processes. Redox stabilization and lipid substrate modulation are therefore approached as parallel axes rather than sequential steps, acknowledging that meaningful system-level stability requires addressing both domains simultaneously.

1. Independent Literature Review

  • Analysis of peer-reviewed research related to axonal degeneration and myelin biology.
  • Review of mechanistic research relevant to very long-chain fatty acid metabolism.
  • Research into the biology of the adult nervous system, including its structural limitations, regenerative constraints, and the realistic potential for functional recovery or structural repair.
  • Evaluation of translational limits between animal models and human data.

2. Mechanism Mapping

  • Identification of oxidative and mitochondrial stress pathways that reduce axonal safety margin and inhibit oligodendrocyte stability.
  • Examination of lipid metabolism and VLCFA accumulation mechanisms affecting membrane integrity and myelin structure.
  • Formal separation of the two intervention axes that define the dual-mechanism model: (1) redox and metabolic stabilization — supported by the triple antioxidant protocol (N-Acetylcysteine, R-Alpha Lipoic Acid, and Vitamin E) — and (2) lipid substrate support and VLCFA modulation — addressed through Nervonic Acid supplementation.
  • Deliberate distinction between preservation strategies and exploratory structural support.

3. Supplier Identification and Process Transparency Review

  • Determination of the botanical source material for Nervonic Acid (Acer truncatum).
  • Research to identify a biotech supplier with documented production standards.
  • Direct communication with the manufacturer to verify production standards, confirm extraction methodology, and request supporting documentation.
  • Review of extraction methodology to confirm process quality and consistency with claimed specifications.
  • Review of available process documentation for internal consistency.

4. Third-Party Laboratory Testing Plan

  • Fatty acid profile confirmation.
  • Purity testing.
  • Heavy metal testing (ICP-MS).
  • Residual solvent analysis.
  • All testing completed and verified through an independent accredited laboratory prior to use.

5. Cost Modeling and Practical Feasibility

  • Estimating the cost of each step required to conduct this project responsibly.
  • Comparing retail versus direct sourcing pathways.
  • Concluding that direct sourcing provided significantly greater long-term feasibility.

6. Legal and Regulatory Review

  • Consultation regarding how the project is represented — confirming it is structured as personal research, not a commercial or clinical operation.
  • Clarification on import classification for raw botanical-derived materials to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.
  • Review of labeling and disclosure practices to confirm transparency and accuracy in how materials and their intended use are described.

7. Data Infrastructure Development

  • Purpose-built daily tracking application designed to capture supplement adherence, morning and evening vitals, symptom severity across nine neurological domains (including gait, spasticity, neurologic fatigue, and cognitive function), pain mapping, psychological state, adverse events, confounders, and structured functional assessments including timed walk and sit-to-stand testing. Includes integrated laboratory tracking for standard panels, VLCFA levels, and RBC membrane fatty acid analysis — a measure of fatty acid incorporation into cell membranes over time. All data feeds into longitudinal trend analysis and structured PDF reporting across all protocol phases.
  • Structured PDF export system for documentation and longitudinal pattern analysis.
  • Cloud-based data storage ensuring all protocol records persist independently of browser or device, with encrypted backup and cross-device access.

🛡️ Dose Escalation Rules Tap to review before any escalation decision
  • Fixed starting dose: 1 g/day NA for the full year-one protocol. No mid-trial adjustments.
  • 12-month minimum: Escalation review cannot open before Day 365. Year one is the 1 g experiment.
  • 30-day contamination window: Any dose change restarts the interpretability clock. Signals during transition periods are uninterpretable.
  • Biomarkers alone are not enough: VLCFA improvement without durable functional improvement does not justify escalation.
  • No obligation to escalate: There is no protocol requirement to increase dose. The default outcome of year-one review is maintain current dose.

Unknown optimal dosing does not justify premature escalation. If year one is broken early, the clean answer is lost.

The AMN Protocol Roadmap — Year One

A structured observation protocol: a flexible baseline period without Nervonic Acid (NA), followed by a 365-day NA treatment protocol. The baseline period provides a clean comparison point so any changes after introducing NA can be measured against your true pre-treatment data.

Baseline Observation — No NA (Variable Length)

Goal: Establish a clean baseline without Nervonic Acid (NA) supplementation. This period captures your natural symptom patterns, vitals, and functional performance so future changes can be measured against a true pre-treatment baseline.

  • No NA supplementation: Do not begin Nervonic Acid during this phase. Continue any other existing supplements as usual—the goal is to isolate NA as the variable introduced in Phase 1.
  • Daily logging: Record energy, cognition, gait, pain, mood, sleep quality, and any adverse or positive events every day. Consistency here is critical—this is your comparison dataset.
  • Vitals: Track weight, blood pressure, heart rate, and temperature as available.
  • Baseline labs: Draw the full baseline panel before starting NA:
    • Hematology: CBC
    • Liver: AST, ALT, ALP, Bilirubin
    • Kidney: Creatinine, eGFR
    • Electrolytes: Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, Bicarbonate
    • Lipids: Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides
    • Disease marker: VLCFA panel
    • RBC fatty acid profile
  • Functional tests: Begin biweekly timed functional tests (e.g., 6MW, 5x sit-to-stand) to establish a performance baseline with multiple data points.
  • Confounder tracking: Log sleep quality, stress, diet changes, and any other factors that could influence symptoms.

Exit criteria: Consistent baseline logging without NA. Baseline labs drawn. Ready to introduce Nervonic Acid.

Phase 1: Tolerability & NA Introduction (Days 1–30)

Goal: Introduce Nervonic Acid (NA) and confirm the full supplement stack is well-tolerated. Compare early data against baseline.

  • Begin NA supplementation: Start Nervonic Acid at the planned dose. Log dose, timing, and any immediate reactions.
  • Daily logging: Continue recording all domains. Watch for any changes from your baseline patterns.
  • Supplement adherence: Log each supplement taken (dose, timing). Mark any missed doses or changes.
  • Adverse event capture: Flag anything new or unusual, even if minor. Compare against baseline—is this new since starting NA?
  • Functional tests: Continue biweekly timed tests. Compare early trends against baseline.

Exit criteria: 30 days on NA with consistent logging. No unresolved safety concerns. Tolerability confirmed.

Phase 2: Monitoring & Follow-up Labs (Days 31–90)

Goal: With tolerability established, watch for early treatment signals and complete the critical 3-month lab panel.

  • Continue daily logging: Same domains. Consistency matters—the value is in the trend line, not any single day.
  • Pattern review: Review the data for emerging trends vs baseline. Is energy trending up? Gait stability improving or declining? Pain shifting?
  • Supplement adjustments: If Phase 1 was clean, consider any planned dose adjustments. Log changes carefully.
  • Functional tests: Continue biweekly schedule. Compare against baseline and Phase 1 trends.
  • 3-month labs (~Day 90): Repeat the full panel (CBC, liver, kidney, electrolytes, lipids, VLCFA). This is your first objective measurement of metabolic response to NA.

Exit criteria: 90 days on NA. 3-month labs drawn. Initial signal assessment documented.

Phase 3: Maintenance (Days 91–270)

Goal: Sustained daily logging through the steady-state period. Monitor for gradual trends, periodic lab checkpoints at 6 and 9 months.

  • Continue daily logging: Maintain consistency. The long-term dataset is the deliverable.
  • Trend monitoring: Periodically review rolling averages against baseline. Look for sustained directional changes rather than daily noise.
  • 6-month labs (~Day 180): Repeat full panel. Compare against baseline and 3-month values for trajectory.
  • 9-month labs (~Day 270): Repeat full panel. Three post-NA lab panels now provide a meaningful trend line.
  • Functional tests: Continue biweekly schedule throughout maintenance.

Exit criteria: 270 days on NA. 6-month and 9-month labs complete. Stable trend data accumulated.

Phase 4: Dose Evaluation Window (Days 271–365)

Goal: Complete year one. Draw 12-month labs. Build enough evidence to make an informed decision on year two.

  • Continue daily logging: Push through to 365 days.
  • Deep trend analysis: Compare year-one averages against baseline (no NA). This is the key comparison—what changed after introducing NA?
  • 12-month labs: Repeat full panel. Compare against all prior panels for objective biomarker trajectory over the full year.
  • Functional tests: Continue biweekly schedule. Year-end data provides a comprehensive trend line.
  • Export & review: Generate the full year-one clinical export. Prepare a summary for your medical team if applicable.
  • Decision point: Review the full year-one dataset for durability, safety, biomarker engagement, and quality of functional trend. A year-one review does not imply dose increase. The default outcome is to maintain the current dose unless predefined escalation criteria are met. Possible outcomes: maintain current dose, defer any change, or open a formal escalation review if criteria are satisfied.

Exit criteria: 365 days complete. Full dataset exported. Year-two decision documented.

Success in year one is a clean, interpretable dataset — not necessarily visible improvement.
